# Week 34 — Kaggle Sprint: Active Competition
## Phase 4: Pre-Competition Peak (Week 34 of 40)

---

## Objective

Join an **active** Kaggle competition (with a live leaderboard) and compete seriously for one week. The goal is the iteration speed and decision-making, not the final rank.

---

## How to Choose a Competition

- Pick a domain you're weakest in (use Week 28 weak area diagnosis)
- Avoid competitions that ended >6 months ago (leaderboard inflation)
- Prefer: tabular, CV classification, or NLP — the most common IOAI formats
- If no active competition: join a featured playground competition

---

## Week Strategy

### Day 1–2: Orientation
- Download data, run EDA
- Implement your baseline template (from Week 31)
- First submission — establish position on leaderboard

### Day 3–4: Rapid Iteration
- Read top 5 public notebooks (for ideas, not to copy directly)
- Try 3 different approaches; record scores
- Focus on fast experiments: change one thing at a time

### Day 5–6: Refinement
- Best model from experiments → tune with Optuna or manual grid search
- Try: feature engineering improvements, model ensembling
- Target: top 20% on leaderboard

### Day 7: Documentation
- What worked? What didn't?
- How long did your first baseline take? (Target: <30 min)
- What would you do in 90 minutes if this were IOAI?

---

## IOAI Mindset During Kaggle

Kaggle normally rewards 2-week optimizations. Simulate the IOAI constraint:
- Pretend you only have 90 minutes per day
- Each day = one 90-min competition session
- Track: what was your score at 30 min? 60 min? 90 min?

- Kaggle competition profile link showing submission history
- `week34_competition_notes.md`:
  - Score timeline (30/60/90 min each day)
  - 3 techniques that worked
  - Final leaderboard rank
