# Week 51 — Competition Week (Pre-Competition)
## Phase 6: Competition Month (Week 51 of 52)

---

## If At-Home Task is Assigned (IOAI Format)

Some IOAI formats assign at-home tasks in advance of the on-site competition. If you receive one:

### Strategy
- Treat it as your most important deliverable of the year
- Follow your competition playbook: **baseline → iterate → document reasoning**
- Submit early versions; improve iteratively (early submission = insurance score)
- Keep detailed notes on every experiment with its score

### At-Home Task Workflow
```
Day 1: Baseline — submit a working solution within 90 min
Day 2: Analysis — deep EDA, understand data structure
Day 3: Improvements — implement top 2 ideas
Day 4: Tuning — optimize best approach with Optuna
Day 5: Ensemble — if time permits, combine top models
Day 6: Polish — clean code, verify submission format, final check
Day 7: Submit — final submission + backup copy saved
```

### Key Rule: Submit Early and Often
An early mediocre submission is always better than a late perfect one that has a bug.

---

## If No At-Home Task: Final Maintenance Mode

- Continue Week 50 warm-up level activity
- One 90-min practice session per day maximum
- Review competition logistics one final time
- Prepare your workspace (quiet, good lighting, backup internet)

---

## The Night Before

- Sleep at normal time — do not stay up late "cramming"
- Have your starter kit templates mentally fresh
- Eat well; don't skip meals
- Lay out everything you need for tomorrow

---

## Morning Of Competition

- Wake up at your normal time (don't disrupt sleep)
- Light exercise or walk (15–20 min)
- Good breakfast — enough to last 6 hours
- Arrive/login 15 minutes early
- Do not cram or review code — trust your preparation
