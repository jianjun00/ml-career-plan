# Regeneron ISEF — 36-Week AI/CS Project Plan
## International Science and Engineering Fair

---

## What ISEF Is

Regeneron ISEF is the world's largest pre-college science competition. ~1,800 finalists from 60+ countries compete each May. Total prize pool: **~$9 million**. Top prize: **$100,000** (George D. Yancopoulos Innovator Award).

**Why it matters for admissions**: ISEF finalist status — especially a Grand Award — is one of the strongest signals a high school student can send to MIT, Stanford, Harvard, CMU, and Caltech. Admissions officers have recognized ISEF for 75+ years.

---

## The Three-Stage Pathway

```
Local/Regional Affiliated Fair (Jan–Mar)
        ↓
  State/National Fair (some regions)
        ↓
  Regeneron ISEF (May, ~1,800 finalists)
```

You cannot apply directly to ISEF. You must win at a local affiliated fair first.
Find your local fair: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/find-a-fair/

## AI/CS Categories to Target

| Category | Code | Best for |
|----------|------|---------|
| Systems Software | SOFT | Novel algorithms, OS, software architecture |
| Software Design | SFTD | ML applications, cybersecurity, databases, mobile |
| Robotics & Intelligent Machines | ROBO | ML systems (MAC subcategory), robotics, cognitive AI |
| Computational Biology & Bioinformatics | CBIO | ML + medicine/biology (large category, strong special awards) |
| Embedded Systems | EBED | Hardware ML, IoT, sensors |

**Strategic advice**: CBIO is large (more award slots) and heavily supported by special awards from pharma/biotech companies. A strong ML + medical project in CBIO can win more total prize money than the same quality project in SFTD.

## Judging Rubric (Engineering/CS — 100 points)

| Criterion | Points | Key question |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Research Problem | 10 | Is the problem significant? Is prior work reviewed? |
| Design & Methodology | 15 | Is the approach rigorous and appropriate? |
| Execution | 20 | Quality of implementation; reliable data |
| Creativity & Potential Impact | 20 | Is it novel? Real-world impact? |
| **Presentation** | **35** | Poster (10) + **Interview (25)** |

**The interview is decisive.** 25 of 35 presentation points come from a 10–15 minute conversation with judges. They test whether YOU did the work and whether you understand it deeply.

## What Winning Projects Look Like

**Pattern across ISEF 2022–2025 top AI/ML winners**:
1. **Original architecture or algorithm** — not applying existing tools to a known problem
2. **Real, meaningful problem** — medical, environmental, accessibility, scientific
3. **Rigorous evaluation** — proper baselines, ablation studies, statistical significance
4. **Publication-ready** — many have arXiv preprints or accepted papers
5. **Domain depth** — the best CS projects also show deep understanding of the application domain

**2025 examples**:
- SOFT 1st: Novel C²-LSTM for heart murmur detection (Ram Sivaraman)
- ROBO 1st: AI-based exoskeletal control system (Chinmayi Goyal)
- CBIO 1st: End-to-end speech conversion for stutter correction (Qianheng Xu)

**2024 Young Scientist ($75k)**: New algorithm for solving second-order cone programs (Michelle Wei)

## 36-Week Plan Overview

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Phase 1: Foundation | 1–4 | ISEF mechanics, domain survey, reading papers |
| Phase 2: Problem Definition | 5–8 | Research gap, mentor acquisition, research plan |
| Phase 3: Environment Setup | 9–10 | Tools, IRB (if needed), baseline literature |
| Phase 4: Core Research | 11–24 | Primary experimentation, model development, iteration |
| Phase 5: Analysis & Writing | 25–30 | Results analysis, research paper, poster draft |
| Phase 6: Regional Fair Prep | 31–34 | Interview practice, board finalization, submission |
| Phase 7: ISEF Prep | 35–36 | Finalist preparation (if qualified) |
