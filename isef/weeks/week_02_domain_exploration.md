## The ISEF Project Failure Mode to Avoid

The most common reason AI/CS projects fail to win at ISEF:

> "I trained a CNN to classify [cats vs dogs / MNIST / some Kaggle dataset] and got 94% accuracy."

This is a tutorial project, not a research project. ISEF judges expect **a contribution to the field** — something that didn't exist before you did it. The domain exploration week is about finding where you can make a real contribution.

## Five High-Value AI/CS Domains for ISEF 2026

These domains have strong track records of winning and robust special award support:

### 1. Medical / Clinical AI
**Why strong**: Judges respond to real human impact. Special awards from pharma and biomedical companies. CBIO category is large (more award slots).
**What's been done**: EKG analysis, cancer detection, drug discovery, rare disease diagnosis
**What's still open**: Multi-modal clinical data (combining imaging + text + lab values), rare disease datasets, low-resource medical settings, explainability/interpretability of clinical models
**Papers to start**: Search "medical imaging deep learning" on Google Scholar; arXiv cs.CV + q-bio.QM

### 2. Accessibility and Assistive Technology
**Why strong**: ROBO category; strong special awards (ACM, AAAI). Projects often win for societal impact.
**What's been done**: Sign language recognition, speech-to-text, eye-tracking interfaces
**What's still open**: Low-latency real-time models for embedded devices, personalized adaptation for individual users, non-English sign languages, multi-modal input fusion
**Papers to start**: "assistive technology deep learning", "AAC augmentative communication AI"

### 3. Environmental and Climate ML
**Why strong**: Very hot topic for 2026. Strong government and NGO special awards. Interdisciplinary.
**What's been done**: Wildfire prediction, species detection, pollution monitoring, crop yield prediction
**What's still open**: Foundation models for Earth observation, transfer learning across geographic regions, rare event prediction, uncertainty quantification
**Papers to start**: arXiv cs.LG + "climate change" or "remote sensing"; "EarthNet" dataset papers

### 4. Novel ML Architecture / Algorithms
**Why strong**: Wins the top ISEF CS prizes most often (like Michelle Wei's algorithm work in 2024)
**What's been done**: Transformer variants, graph neural networks, self-supervised learning
**What's still open**: Efficient inference on low-power hardware, theoretical bounds for specific problem classes, improving robustness/calibration, new inductive biases
**Papers to start**: arXiv cs.LG, ICLR/NeurIPS proceedings; look for "open problems" sections

### 5. Computational Biology and Bioinformatics
**Why strong**: CBIO is the largest ISEF category. Intersection of ML + biology is exploding.
**What's been done**: Protein structure prediction (AlphaFold-adjacent), genomics classification, drug-target interaction
**What's still open**: Single-cell analysis, RNA structure prediction, metabolomics, microbiome analysis
**Papers to start**: Nature Methods, PLOS Computational Biology, bioRxiv; "machine learning genomics 2024"

## How to Read a Research Paper (Efficiently)

For survey reading this week, use the **3-pass method**:

**Pass 1 (5 min)**: Title, abstract, section headers, conclusion. Decide: is this relevant?
**Pass 2 (20 min)**: Introduction, figures, results table, discussion. What problem, what method, what result?
**Pass 3 (60 min, only for key papers)**: Full read. Understand every design choice.

For domain exploration, Pass 1 + Pass 2 is enough. Save Pass 3 for your chosen domain's foundational papers.
