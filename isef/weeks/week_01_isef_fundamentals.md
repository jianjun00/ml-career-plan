## Required Reading (6 hours)

### 1. Official Rules (2 hours)
- **Rules for All Projects**: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/international-rules/rules-for-all-projects/
- **Display and Safety Rules**: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/international-rules/display-safety-rules/
- Read the full **2026 ISEF Rules Booklet** — download from https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/forms/
- **Why**: Ignorance of rules is not an excuse. Projects are disqualified at fairs for form violations. Know Form 1, 1A, 1B before you touch any data.

### 2. Categories and Judging (1 hour)
- **All Categories**: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/categories-and-subcategories/all-categories/
- **Grand Award Judging Criteria**: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/grand-award/criteria/
- **Awards Overview**: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/awards/
- Task: Read the judging rubric carefully. Write down in your own words what judges are testing in the interview.

### 3. Find Your Local Fair (1 hour)
- **Find-a-Fair Database**: https://www.societyforscience.org/isef/find-a-fair/
- Find the fair nearest to you. Record:
  - Fair name and website
  - Deadline to register
  - Date of the fair
  - Number of projects that advance to ISEF
  - Category quotas (how many projects per category advance?)

### 4. Read 3 Winning Project Abstracts (2 hours)
Past ISEF project summaries: https://abstracts.societyforscience.org/
- Find 3 top-placing AI/CS projects from 2023–2025 (categories: SOFT, ROBO, SFTD, CBIO)
- For each, write one paragraph: What was the problem? What was novel? What evaluation did they do?
- **2025 examples to start with**:
  - SOFT057: HM-Detect (C²-LSTM for heart murmur detection)
  - CBIO winner: StutterZero (speech conversion)
  - ROBO winner: MyoAssist (AI exoskeletal control)

## Key Rules to Know Cold

### Forms Required for ALL Projects
| Form | When | What |
|------|------|------|
| **Research Plan** | BEFORE experimentation | Detailed written plan: rationale, methods, risk assessment, bibliography |
| **Form 1 (Adult Sponsor Checklist)** | BEFORE experimentation | Adult sponsor reviews and signs off |
| **Form 1A (Student Checklist)** | BEFORE experimentation | Student self-certification |
| **Form 1B (Approval Form)** | BEFORE experimentation | Signed confirmation of review |
| **Abstract (250 words)** | After experimentation, before fair | Describes only current year's work |

### When IRB/SRC Approval Is Required (must be done BEFORE any data collection)
- Human participants (surveys, behavioral data, biometric data, images of people)
- Vertebrate animal research
- Potentially hazardous biological agents
- Human or animal tissue

**Critical rule**: IRB/SRC approval cannot be given retroactively. If you collect data before approval, your project is ineligible.

### AI Tools Policy
AI tools (ChatGPT, etc.) may be used as research tools but CANNOT write the research plan, abstract, research paper, or poster content. Must be cited if used in the research itself.

## Category Selection

Evaluate which category fits the project you're likely to pursue. Use this decision tree:

```
Is your project primarily about novel ML algorithms or architectures?
  → SOFT (Systems Software) or ROBO subcategory MAC (Machine Learning)

Is your application domain biology, medicine, or health?
  → CBIO (Computational Biology) — larger category, more award slots

Is your project a software application (app, tool, interface)?
  → SFTD (Software Design) subcategory APP or HMC

Is your project hardware + ML (sensor, IoT, embedded system)?
  → EBED (Embedded Systems)

Is the core contribution a mathematical algorithm or theory?
  → MATH (Mathematics)
```

**Tip**: You can switch categories up until you submit to your regional fair. Choose by where your project is strongest, not where you think there's less competition.
